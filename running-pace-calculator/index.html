Complete Running & Race Pace Calculator Guide

What is Running & Race Pace?

Running pace and race pace are essential metrics for runners, similar to what you see in Strava. Whether you're training or racing, understanding your pace (minutes per kilometer or mile) helps optimize your performance and achieve your goals.

How to Use Our Advanced Pace Calculator

  1. Choose your race or run distance (5K, 10K, Half Marathon, or Marathon)
  2. Input your target finish time or desired pace
  3. Get instant pace calculations for training and racing
  4. Use our Strava-style insights to plan your training
  5. Save and share your calculated paces

Common Running Paces

  • Beginner Marathon: 5:30-6:30/km (8:51-10:27/mile)
  • Intermediate Half Marathon: 5:00-5:30/km (8:03-8:51/mile)
  • Advanced 10K: 4:00-4:30/km (6:26-7:14/mile)
  • Elite 5K: 3:00-3:30/km (4:50-5:38/mile)

Frequently Asked Questions

What pace should I run for my first marathon?

For your first marathon, aim for a comfortable pace that's about 1 minute slower per kilometer than your half marathon pace. Use our calculator to find the right pace based on your target finish time.

How do I calculate my running pace?

To calculate your running pace manually, divide your total time in minutes by the distance. Our pace calculator automates this process and provides both km and mile paces.

What's a good running pace for beginners?

Beginners should focus on maintaining a comfortable pace where they can hold a conversation. This typically means 6:00-7:00/km (9:39-11:16/mile) for general training runs.

How can I improve my running pace?

Improve your pace through structured training including speed work, tempo runs, and long runs. Use our calculator to set progressive pace goals throughout your training.
